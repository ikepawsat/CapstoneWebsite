Historic Information

Previous qualifying-time changes

The Boston Athletic Association (B.A.A.) first published age-group qualifying standards for male runners in 1970 and for female runners in 1972. Since then the B.A.A. has adjusted those standards multiple times — for example, a major tightening occurred in 2011 when qualifying times were reduced (made faster) by roughly five minutes across the age groups, and in later years the 18–34 standard was shifted from 3:05:00 down to 3:00:00 for men (with comparable changes for women). The B.A.A. also continues to refine registration and acceptance rules to manage field size and fairness.

Year (effective) What changed Notes
1970 / 1972 Qualifying standards published (men 1970; women 1972) B.A.A. established age-group standards.
2011 (for 2013 registration) Qualifying times tightened (~5 minutes faster across groups) Also introduced a registration order system prioritizing faster qualifiers.
2018 (announced for 2020 race) 18–34 men lowered from 3:05:00 → 3:00:00 (women similarly shifted) Part of multi-year adjustments to standards.
2025/2026 Standards tightened for 2026 (e.g., many under-60 groups moved 5 minutes faster) B.A.A. adjusted the published standards for the 2026 field. See sources.
2025 (policy update) Time adjustments for downhill courses Times run on courses with ≥1,500 ft net downhill may incur an adjustment (e.g., ≥5 minutes).
